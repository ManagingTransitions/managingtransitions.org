Output-to-Outcome Pathway

A draft results chain, not a finished one.

Adapted from the standard results-chain framework used across WHO and OECD monitoring systems (inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact). This is a first pass, meant to be reworked jointly with TS and the Ministries of Health in Phase 1, not a fixed model.

Inputs

Infrastructure

EVC-Plus / ZAAD rails, IVR system, community health worker network

→
Activities

Delivery

Consultations conducted, follow-up calls placed, referrals issued

→
Outputs

Countable

# consultations, # follow-ups completed at 30/60/90 days, # referrals, # mobile visits triggered

→
Outcomes

Changes observed

Self-reported wellbeing improvement (EQ-5D-style), reduced lost-to-follow-up rate

→
Impact

Longer-term

School attendance among dependents, household economic activity, reduced catastrophic health spend

Women over 50 in rural Bakool are contacting clinics at a third of the rate of every other segment. The system flags this rather than averaging it away, it doesn't explain why on its own. Mobility constraints, device access, and who in the household controls the phone are the first three questions worth taking to the community health workers on the ground, not assumptions to bake into the dashboard.

Who Sees What

The same underlying data, three different interfaces.

District Coordinator

Resource allocation view

Outbreak clustering, DHIS2-format export, and a follow-up backlog queue, built for someone managing supply and staffing across facilities.

Rural Health Worker

Audio-first, minimal text

Short voice prompts rather than forms, for contexts where literacy, connectivity, and available time are all constrained.

Ministry of Health

Auditable, SDG-aligned

Aggregate indicators mapped against SDG and WHO reporting categories, structured to be independently audited, not taken on trust.
