Output-to-Outcome Pathway

A draft results chain, not a finished one.

Adapted from the standard results-chain framework used across WHO and OECD monitoring systems (inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact). A first pass, meant to be reworked jointly with TS and the Ministries of Health in Phase 1.

Inputs

Infrastructure

EVC-Plus / ZAAD rails, IVR system, community health worker network

→
Activities

Delivery

Consultations conducted, follow-up calls placed, referrals issued

→
Outputs

Countable

# consultations, # follow-ups completed, # referrals, # mobile visits triggered

→
Outcomes

Changes observed

Self-reported wellbeing improvement (EQ-5D-style), reduced lost-to-follow-up rate

→
Impact

Longer-term

School attendance among dependents, household economic activity, reduced catastrophic health spend

Monitoring & Evaluation Dashboard

One screen: what's working, what needs attention, what to do next.

+0.15
Average wellbeing improvement across both platforms, programme to date. 953 referrals issued and 7,877 people reached in the last 90 days.
WinFollow-up completion is up 13 points since Period 1, and data completeness is rising with it, so the gain is real, not just better counting.
WatchOnly 57% of referrals end in a recorded outcome. People are attending; the record-keeping after the visit is where most of the loss happens.
ActionPrioritise community health worker home visits over SMS reminders next quarter, they cost more per contact but move wellbeing roughly ten times as much.

Who Sees What

The same underlying data, three different interfaces.

District Coordinator

Resource allocation view

Outbreak clustering, DHIS2-format export, and a follow-up backlog queue.

Rural Health Worker

Audio-first, minimal text

Short voice prompts rather than forms, for constrained literacy, connectivity, and time.

Ministry of Health

Auditable, SDG-aligned

Aggregate indicators mapped against SDG and WHO categories, built to be audited, not taken on trust.
