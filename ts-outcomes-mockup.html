This is the core design decision from the concept note made visible: a patient who also sees a traditional healer or elder is recorded as a data point about how care is actually sought, not quietly counted as a failure of the platform. The record stays useful whether the patient followed one care path or several.

Who Sees What

The same underlying data, three different interfaces.

District Coordinator

Resource allocation view

Outbreak and early-warning clustering, DHIS2-format export, and a "who's falling behind on follow-up" queue, built for someone managing supply and staffing across multiple facilities, not for reading individual records.

Rural Health Worker

Audio-first, minimal text

A simplified mobile view built around short voice prompts ("How is the baby?") rather than forms, for context where literacy, connectivity, and available time are all constrained. Complexity is hidden, not removed.

Ministry of Health

Auditable, SDG-aligned

A credibility-first view: aggregate indicators mapped against SDG and WHO reporting categories, structured so it can be independently audited rather than taken on trust.
