Output-to-Outcome Pathway

A draft results chain, not a finished one.

Adapted from the standard results-chain framework used across WHO and OECD monitoring systems (inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact). A first pass, meant to be reworked jointly with TS and the Ministries of Health in Phase 1.

Infrastructure

EVC-Plus / ZAAD rails, IVR system, community health worker network

→

→

School attendance among dependents, household economic activity, reduced catastrophic health spend

Monitoring & Evaluation Dashboard

Is the programme being delivered well, and can the data be trusted.

Five things worth tracking at programme-management level, distinct from population health outcomes: delivery fidelity, referral conversion, data quality, whether coverage gaps are closing, and whether the two platforms are converging or diverging.

1 · Follow-Up Completion Over Time

Is the rate improving as the programme matures.

30-day completion 90-day completion
30-day: 58% → 71% across six periods. 90-day: 42% → 58%. Both rising, 90-day still lagging 30-day by a wide margin.

2 · Referral Conversion

72% of referrals result in an attended visit. Of those, 79% get an outcome actually recorded, the second drop-off, not the first, is where most of the loss happens.

3 · Data Completeness & Quality

Whether the data itself is getting more reliable.

Full EQ-5D set recorded Duplicate shared-phone IDs resolved
Full EQ-5D capture: 61% → 83%. Duplicate ID resolution: 52% → 80%. Both improving, worth watching rather than assuming, since a rising wellbeing score means less if completeness is also rising from a low base.

Who Sees What

The same underlying data, three different interfaces.

District Coordinator

Resource allocation view

Outbreak clustering, DHIS2-format export, and a follow-up backlog queue.

Rural Health Worker

Audio-first, minimal text

Short voice prompts rather than forms, for constrained literacy, connectivity, and time.

Ministry of Health

Auditable, SDG-aligned

Aggregate indicators mapped against SDG and WHO categories, built to be audited, not taken on trust.
