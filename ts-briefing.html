Capturing Outcomes in an Emerging, Parallel Health System

Baano (Somalia) & Shaafi (Somaliland): From Transactional Outputs to Longitudinal Impact

Prepared by Matt Thomson PhD Researcher, Politics of Localisation, University of Bristol July 2026

The Opportunity

Two platforms, hundreds of thousands of patients, no outcomes evidence yet.

Baano and Shaafi ride the Horn of Africa's most resilient infrastructure, Hormuud's EVC-Plus in Mogadishu and Telesom's ZAAD in Hargeisa, reaching patients across a sovereignty divide that few institutions can bridge. But both platforms currently measure activity, not impact: calls completed, consults booked, prescriptions issued.

For corporate health partners, Ministries of Health, and donors increasingly funding outcomes rather than outputs, this gap limits the platforms' commercial defensibility and undersells the asset Transparency Solutions has already built.

This note proposes a Somali-led Outcomes Conversion Engine: a logical framework and technical specification that converts a single IVR call or EVC-Plus consult into longitudinal, trustworthy evidence of patient wellbeing, without requiring Somalia and Somaliland to become the same system.

The Risk of Standing Still

Every month spent output-only leaves Baano and Shaafi's strongest asset, proof of impact, unmeasured and unmonetised, weakening their position with the corporate and donor partners who will fund the platforms' next phase of growth only against outcomes they can trust.

Three Pillars of the Design Sprint

Tap each pillar for the detail.

01

Population-Level Outcomes Architecture

+
An aggregate outcomes layer, distinct from either platform's patient management system, built for disaggregation by gender, age, geography, and displacement status (IDP vs. host community). Designed for DHIS2-compatible export, so the engine strengthens Ministry of Health reporting rather than competing with it.
02

Patient-Level Longitudinal Integration

+
A unique patient-ID architecture (resolving the shared-phone problem) tracking wellbeing at 30/60/90-day, or longer, intervals. Built on contribution, not attribution: a patient who also consults a traditional healer or elder is a data point, not a dropout, reflecting how care is genuinely sought across Somalia and Somaliland.
03

Technical Brief & Dual-Export Specification

+
A "Single Core, Dual Export" model that treats Somalia's app-first/EVC-Plus environment and Somaliland's IVR-first/ZAAD environment as genuinely distinct regulatory and technical contexts. Includes a brief for a locally co-produced outcome measure (a Somali-language equivalent to EQ-5D, developed rather than imported) and mobile-money-linked micro-compensation for completed follow-ups.

Grounded in Somali First Principles

All outcome data remains Somali-owned. Longitudinal re-contact requires its own explicit consent, never assumed from a one-off consult. The framework is designed for Maxaa, Maay, and Bravanese from the outset, and all community consultation and codesign is led by Transparency Solutions' Somali field teams, not by external consultants.

Phase 1 Deliverables

  • Outcomes Conversion Matrix, covering both population- and patient-level metrics
  • Dual-Export Technical Specification for Somalia/EVC-Plus and Somaliland/ZAAD
  • Localised Outcome Measure design brief (Somali-language, co-produced)
  • Data Governance & Consent Framework
  • Stakeholder Engagement Plan (ministries, hospitals, pharmacy distributors, community health workers)

Delivery Model

12 to 18 days of scoped work, delivered over 8 to 12 weeks.

Initiation

Systems audit & consultation on priorities

Systems Design

Population-level model, patient-level integration, technical brief and SOPs

Review

Consultation with health ministries, frontline health workers, and relevant community and clan groups

Distributed co-design: Transparency Solutions' Somali field teams lead all community consultation at the pace the context requires. Weekly synthesis is delivered against TS's Thursday reporting cycle, respecting Friday as a day of rest, cadence, not just content, reflecting Somali First values.

Optional Phase 2 (Implementation) and Phase 3 (Management) are scoped separately once Phase 1 architecture is agreed.
